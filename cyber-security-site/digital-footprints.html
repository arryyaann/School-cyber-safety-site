Digital Footprints

Your digital footprint is the trace you leave online from what you do, such as the emails you send, the things you post, and pretty much everything you do.

Many people think by deleting what they don’t want people to find, it’s gone but that’s not true. Once you post something on the internet it’s there forever, even if you think it’s gone.

Types and Significance

There are two types that digital footprints are characterised into:

  • Passive
  • Active

Passive footprints are those that are left by users unknowingly, such as when visiting a website that is tracking you through cookies. [fragments of user data used to improve browsing experience.]

Active footprints are otherwise created when users deliberately share information through online profiles, on blogs or by uploading photos. The impacts of digital footprints can be immense.

They can affect privacy and security. For example, personal information shared online can be harvested by cybercriminals for identity theft, phishing attacks, or other malicious activities. Companies can also be held accountable for using digital footprints for targeted advertising, which raises concerns about data privacy that is accessible by these companies.
